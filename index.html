Current focus

Elegant,
but not loud.

The Seedcan is designed around three constraints: it must be buildable, cleanable, and operable by someone who has never run a bioreactor before.

Material reality v1 scope

Food-safe, affordable, durable, and easy to clean at home. Borosilicate glass vessel, 316L stainless steel hardware, platinum-cured silicone seals. No exotic materials, no compromises on safety.

Control simplicity v1 scope

Temperature, pH, and gentle agitation — only as much control as the ferment truly needs. An ESP32 PID loop handles heating. A single button starts and stops a batch. The rest is biology.

Human fit v1 scope

Countertop ergonomics. Visible fermentation through the glass vessel. Disassembles into dishwasher-safe parts. Designed to belong in a kitchen, not a lab.

Principles

I
Feasibility before elegance. The device must be provably buildable before it becomes beautiful. Every design choice is tested against: can this be made, cleaned, and operated reliably?
II
Natural microbes only. No GMO scope — at any stage. The Seedcan works with the biology that already exists in food culture: lactobacillus, acetobacter, yeast, SCOBY.
III
Minimum necessary control. The device controls what it must — temperature, pH, agitation — and nothing more. Complexity is a liability in a kitchen appliance.
IV
Scope discipline. Commercialization, solid substrates, dissolved oxygen sensing, and app connectivity are all real ideas — and all deliberately out of v1 scope.
